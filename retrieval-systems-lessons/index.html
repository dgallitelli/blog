5 Lessons from Building Production Retrieval Systems with AI

Feb 24, 2026
AI RAG production

After helping dozens of enterprise customers build retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) systems, patterns start to emerge. Here are five lessons that keep coming up.

1. Chunking strategy matters more than you think

Most teams start with fixed-size chunks and never revisit the decision. But the way you split your documents has an outsized impact on retrieval quality. Semantic chunking, recursive splitting, and document-aware strategies each have their place — and the right choice depends on your corpus.

2. Evaluation is not optional

If you can't measure retrieval quality, you can't improve it. Build evaluation into your pipeline from day one. Use metrics like recall@k, MRR, and NDCG — and complement them with LLM-as-a-judge for answer quality.

3. Embeddings are not one-size-fits-all

General-purpose embeddings work surprisingly well, but domain-specific fine-tuning can unlock significant gains. The key is knowing when the effort is worth it — and having the evaluation framework to prove it.

4. Hybrid search wins

Pure vector search has blind spots. Combining dense retrieval with sparse methods (BM25, keyword matching) consistently outperforms either approach alone. The fusion strategy matters — reciprocal rank fusion is a solid default.

5. The last mile is the hardest

Getting from "works in a notebook" to "reliable in production" is where most projects stall. Caching, latency budgets, fallback strategies, and monitoring are not glamorous — but they're what separates a demo from a product.

The best retrieval system is the one your users trust. And trust is built through consistency, not cleverness.
